Atomic structure

Electricity involves the movement of free electrons. In copper conductors, electrons in the outer shell are loosely held and can move through the material when an electromotive force is applied.

Revision point: Conventional current is treated as flowing from positive to negative, even though electron flow is from negative to positive.

EMF generation

Electromotive force can be generated in several ways:

  • Chemically: cells and batteries.
  • Magnetically: generators and alternators.
  • Thermally: thermocouples.

Ohm’s Law

Power formulas

Series circuits

  • Total resistance: RTotal = R1 + R2 + ...
  • Current is the same everywhere.
  • Voltage divides across components.

Parallel circuits

  • Total resistance: 1/RTotal = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...
  • Voltage is the same across each branch.
  • Current divides between branches.

Magnetic flux

Magnetic flux is the total magnetic field passing through an area.

Φ = magnetic flux, measured in Webers Wb

Flux density

Flux density is flux per unit area.

B = Φ ÷ A, measured in Tesla T

What is Ohm’s Law?
V = I × R. Voltage equals current multiplied by resistance.
What does Cg correct for?
Grouping. It accounts for cables heating each other when installed together.
What is the design coordination equation?
Ib ≤ In ≤ Iz.
What is Zs?
Earth fault loop impedance: Zs = Ze + (R1 + R2).
